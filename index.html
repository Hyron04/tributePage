Sir Albert Einstein

Here's a time line of Albert Einstein's life:

  • 1879 - Born in Ulm, Germany.
  • 1900 - Graduated from the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich.
  • 1902 - Became a patent examiner in Bern, Switzerland.
  • 1904 - Became a Swiss citizen.
  • 1905 - Published the paper that introduced the equation E=mc^2.
  • 1905 - Published the special theory of relativity.
  • 1907 - Became a lecturer at the University of Bern.
  • 1915 - Completed the general theory of relativity.
  • 1919 - Eddington's expedition confirmed the general theory of relativity.
  • 1921 - Became a foreign member of the Royal Netherlands Academy of Arts and Sciences.
  • 1921 - Became a member of the Prussian Academy of Sciences.
  • 1921 - Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics.
  • 1922 - Traveled to Japan and other countries on a lecture tour.
  • 1922 - Awarded the Copley Medal by the Royal Society of London.
  • 1922 - Awarded the Gold Medal of the Royal Astronomical Society.
  • 1933 - Emigrated to the U.S. after being targeted.
  • 1936 - His wife, Elsa Einstein, passed away.
  • 1939 - Signed a letter to President Franklin D. Roosevelt urging the development of an atomic bomb.
  • 1940 - Became a U.S. citizen.
  • 1950 - Published his unified field theory.
  • 1952 - Declined the presidency of Israel.
  • 1953 - Completed his work on the theory of gravitation.
  • 1953 - Awarded the Franklin Medal by the Franklin Institute.
  • 1954 - Declined the offer of the presidency of the Federal Republic of Germany.
  • 1955 - Died in Princeton, New Jersey.
  • 1965 - The Cosmic Microwave Background Radiation was discovered, providing evidence for the Big Bang theory.
  • 1974 - Stephen Hawking proposed that black holes emit radiation, known as Hawking radiation, based on Einstein's theory of general relativity.
  • 1993 - The Nobel Prize in Physics was awarded to Russell Hulse and Joseph Taylor for the discovery of a new type of pulsar, providing further evidence for the existence of gravitational waves predicted by Einstein.
  • 2015 - The LIGO Scientific Collaboration announced the first direct detection of gravitational waves, confirming a major prediction of Einstein's general theory of relativity.
  • 2019 - The Event Horizon Telescope collaboration released the first image of a black hole, further validating Einstein's theories.
  • 2020 - The Nobel Prize in Physics was awarded to Roger Penrose for the discovery that black hole formation is a robust prediction of the general theory of relativity.

"The important thing is not to stop questioning. Curiosity has its own reason for existing."

-- Albert Einstein
